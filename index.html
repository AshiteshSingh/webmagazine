Words that Weave Our Journey

Jobi Joseph - Chief Editor

On this blessed occasion of Gandhi Jayanti and the feast of our patron, Saint Thérèse of the Child Jesus, the Little Flower, I extend my heartfelt greetings and warm wishes to the entire school community. These two radiant days remind us of the fragrance of simplicity, the strength of truth, and the power of love—values that remain ever-relevant to our journey as students and educators.

It is with immense joy and pride that we unveil our Digital School Magazine for the academic year 2025–26. This endeavour is not merely a collection of articles, poems, paintings, and reports; it is the living testimony of the creativity, dedication, and collective spirit that flow through our school corridors. Each page breathes the imagination of our students, the guidance of our teachers, and the vision of our leaders.

I record my sincere gratitude to our Manager, Rev. Fr Dominic, our Principal, Rev. Fr Sabu, and our Vice Principal, Rev. Fr Anish for their wise counsel and steadfast encouragement. Their vision has been the guiding lamp of this venture. To our teachers, who nurtured and inspired every contribution with patience and care, I extend my deepest appreciation. My heartfelt thanks also to the Editorial Board and the School Council editorial team, whose tireless efforts and sharp eyes for detail have brought forth a work of beauty and substance.

A very special word of admiration must be given to our Class 11 students, Ashitesh and Aashay, who have developed this digital magazine with remarkable skill and perseverance. Their craftsmanship, creativity, and unwavering commitment have breathed life into this platform and stand as a shining example of youthful brilliance and teamwork.

Above all, I cherish every contribution made by our students—be it a line of poetry, a stroke of colour, or a thoughtful reflection. It is your voices that give this magazine its soul.

May this digital venture not only delight and inspire but also strengthen in us the resolve to live in truth, simplicity, and love—values exemplified by Mahatma Gandhi and Saint Thérèse, the Little Flower.

The Little Way to a Great Life

Shamen Vijay Ronald- Associate Editor

October 1st is the feast day of St. Thérèse of Lisieux, known lovingly as the "Little Flower of Jesus." Her life, though short and hidden within a Carmelite convent, offers a powerful message that resonates deeply with young people today. She reminds us that holiness isn't just for saints who perform extraordinary miracles but for each one of us in our everyday lives.

Embracing the Little Way

St. Thérèse called her spiritual path the "Little Way," a journey of finding God and a great life through small acts of love. She understood that we don't have to change the world in a single, grand gesture. Instead, we can transform our own lives and the lives of those around us by doing ordinary things with extraordinary love.

This message is especially powerful for all of us dear students, as it encourages us to find purpose and joy in our everyday lives—in our duties and studies, in friendships, and in families.

Imagine this: a kind word to a friend, a moment of patience when you're frustrated, a sincere effort in your studies even when they are difficult. These seemingly insignificant actions, when offered with a pure heart, become a powerful prayer. St. Thérèse believed that these small sacrifices are incredibly valuable in God's eyes, like scattering flowers before Him.

"Miss no single opportunity of making some small sacrifice, here by a smiling look, there by a kindly word; always doing the smallest right and doing it all for love." — St. Thérèse of Lisieux

A Legacy of Trust

The core of St. Thérèse's "Little Way" is her profound trust in God's mercy and love. She saw herself as a small child, completely dependent on her heavenly Father. This childlike trust is a beautiful lesson for all of us. It reminds us that we don't have to be perfect. When we make mistakes, we can simply turn to God with humility and trust that His love is greater than our faults.

As we celebrate the feast day of our School's Patron Saint, let us be inspired by the "Little Way." Let us strive to be kind, compassionate, and full of love in all that we do. By embracing this simple yet powerful path, we can truly become the "little flowers" God created us to be, blooming with grace and spreading His love to the world.
